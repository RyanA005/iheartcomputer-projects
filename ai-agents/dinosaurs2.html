Dinosaurs I Love

A short, stylish guide to three favorites — fierce, clever, and iconic.

Tyrannosaurus Rex

The King
Late Cretaceous — North America
  • Massive predator with bone-crushing bite force.
  • Amazing example of convergent evolution and dominance.
  • Popular culture icon — powerful and instantly recognizable.

Velociraptor

Swift
Late Cretaceous — Central Asia
  • Small, fast, and highly intelligent hunters (feathered).
  • Pack hunting is debated, but agility was its hallmark.
  • Famous thanks to movies, but the real animal was feathered and smaller.

Stegosaurus

Plated
Late Jurassic — North America
  • Recognizable by its plates and spiked tail (thagomizer).
  • Herbivore with a modest brain and impressive defenses.
  • A reminder that not all remarkable dinosaurs were predators.
